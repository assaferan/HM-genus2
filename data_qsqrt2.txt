Notes
===========================================================================

Convention for L-polynomials:
  L_p(T) = 1 + a1*T + a2*T^2 + a3*T^3 + N(p)^2 * T^4
  with a3 = N(p)*a1 (from functional equation) and |a1| <= 4*sqrt(N(p)).
  The number of F_{N(p)}-points on Jac(C) is L_p(1) = 1+a1+a2+a3+N(p)^2.
  Factorization is over F_25[T] = GF(5^2)[T].  The generator w25 of GF(5^2)
  satisfies $.1^2 + 4*$.1 + 2 (Conway polynomial).

Rational (1,5)-polarization structure:
  The HM search finds abelian surfaces A = Jac(C) carrying a K-rational
  (1,5)-polarization; equivalently, a Lagrangian 5^2-subgroup scheme defined
  over K.  At the (inert) prime above 5 (N=25), this structure implies
  5 | L_{p5}(1) = #Jac(C)(F_25), i.e. T=1 is a root of L_{p5} mod 5.

Galois action:
  Classes come in Galois-conjugate pairs under Gal(K/Q): s2 -> -s2.
  Conjugate curves have L-polynomials that are related by the same s2->-s2
  substitution in the coefficients; for inert or ramified p these coincide.

Notes on reduction failures:
  'reduction failed' in the Frobenius table is a MODEL artifact, not evidence
  of bad reduction.  The Igusa criterion and ConductorExponentAt both confirm
  good reduction at those primes.  The issue: the current Weierstrass model
  (from Genus2CurveFromIgusa, via a random Mestre conic point) can have
  leading coefficient divisible by certain prime ideals; neither integer
  shifts nor the flip x->1/x resolve this.  A minimal Weierstrass model
  at those primes would give good reduction.

Sharing these curves:
  The compact Igusa-Siegel invariants [J2:...:J10] above are the recommended
  form for sharing.  A Weierstrass model y^2 = f(x) over Q(sqrt(2)) can be
  recovered in Magma via:

    import "Genus2Curve.m": Genus2CurveFromIgusa;
