=== honest feasibility assessment ===
- Target F_A is precisely pinned: degree-50 resolvent / PSL_2(F_49) / ramified {2,3,5,7}.
- No genus-2 division polynomial in Magma; algebraic route is frontier-level.
- Numerical foundation (periods) is fine.  BUT:
  * the degree-50 resolvent needs the F_49-structure (the element J defining
    F_49-multiplication on A[7](x)F49) -- this is ARITHMETIC (A has End=Z, so J is
    not a geometric endomorphism), entangled with the field we are computing;
  * the raw degree-400 resolvent (P^3(F_7) lines, no J needed) is computable from
    numerical torsion but large, and recognizing a degree-400 polynomial over
    Q(sqrt10) needs very high precision.
- CONCLUSION: F_A is a genuine numerical project of the same order as F_f; the
  torsion-field route to drop GRH is heavy on BOTH sides for this example.  The
  Serre-conjecture-over-Q(sqrt10) route (needs neither) remains the better bet.

=== numerical pipeline test + the End=Z obstruction (goal2_FA_numprobe.m) ===
AnalyticJacobian(C : Precision:=100) + BigPeriodMatrix + FromAnalyticJacobian:
WORKS -- a 7-torsion point z=(1/7)*lattice-vector maps back to its degree-2 divisor
{P1,P2} on C with recognizable-in-principle complex coordinates.

BUT the clean degree-50 resolvent (matching F_f's PSL_2(F_49) field) needs the
F_49-line structure, i.e. the element J with J^2 = 3 giving F_49-multiplication on
A[7].  J is a mod-7 arithmetic accident of the Galois image, NOT a geometric
endomorphism (End(A)=Z), so it is invisible to the numerical geometry.  Without J
the only resolvents formable from the raw F_7-structure are degree 400 (P^3(F_7)
lines) or 2400 (all torsion) -- too large to recognize over Q(sqrt10).

CRUX: the van Bommel et al. method fundamentally applies to RM abelian varieties.
B_f (form side) HAS RM (deg-18 field) -> F_f structured but dim-18 heavy.  A (curve
side) has End=Z, NO RM -> F_A resolvent blows up.  So the torsion-field comparison
is blocked on BOTH sides, for complementary reasons.  It is the right idea but does
not escape the big-image difficulty for this example.
